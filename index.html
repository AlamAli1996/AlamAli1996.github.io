About Me

I am a Postdoctoral Fellow in the Department of Statistics and Data Science at the Indian Institute of Technology Kanpur. I completed my Ph.D. in Statistics from the Central University of Punjab. My research focuses on developing statistical models for complex data structures with applications in regression analysis, dependence modeling, and statistical inference.

My current research interests include copula-based regression models, path analysis, survival analysis, distribution theory, and robust statistical methods. I am particularly interested in developing flexible statistical approaches that relax classical assumptions and provide better modeling of real-world data.

Research Interests

My research focuses on developing statistical methodologies for modeling complex data structures. My main research interests include:

  • Copula Models: Dependence modeling using Gaussian, Student's t, and Archimedean copulas.
  • Regression Analysis: Development of flexible regression models under non-normality and heteroscedasticity.
  • Path Analysis: Copula-based recursive path models for studying direct, indirect, and total effects.
  • Survival Analysis: Statistical modeling and inference for survival data.
  • Distribution Theory: Development and application of new probability distributions.
  • Statistical Inference: Parameter estimation, asymptotic theory, and robust statistical methods.
